Hi!

It's very nice to have you here! I'm Eduardo, and I have a BSc in Biomedical Engineering and a MSc in the Postgraduate Program in Biomedical Engineering, both at the Federal University of Uberlandia. Currently, I work as an RD&I Engineer at the Laboratory of Technology in Friction and Wear (LTAD) developing instruments for the Oil and Gas industry. As an engineer, I work developing on-demand solutions that involve the design and construction of hardware, firmware, and software. I have experience with instrumentation, biomedical signal processing, deep learning techniques for computer vision and neuromorphic (event-based) vision, web systems development (backend, frontend and database) and embedded systems development.

Projects

Equipament's Calibration System

System to manage the callibration process of equipaments on hospital. In there, the user are able to registrate calibration data of each equipament of the hospital, generate reports and maintain traceability.


Electron Framework, Angular and Node.Js

Pellets Quality Analysis

Here, we developed an mobile app (Android - Ionic) to analyze the dimensions of pellets through image processing (OpenCV) and determinate some process variables that could be interfering on the full productivity of the machinary.


Ionic, Angular, OpenCV and Flask (Python)

3 channels analog signal acquisition

In this project we build a real time software (Windows Form - C#) to visualize and process the analog signal data acquired using the A/D conversor from an Arduino. This system could be used to see the analog signal of any three generic sensors, simultaneously.


Arduino, C# and Real-time signal acquisition

Signal Generator

Any time that we want to simulate an signal and apply a processing step on they, we could use the Signal Generator developed in this educational and didatical project developed during my graduation.


C# and Signal Processing

SSVEP - Steady state visually evoked potential

SSVEP are natural responses to visual stimulation. So, we developed an electroencephalographic equipament to read signals of the visual cortex in the brain and discriminate for each stimulation the user was looking for.


Windows Form - C#, Brain-Computer Interface and Signal Processing

Detection and Tracking of objects recorded using a event-based camera

The use of neuromorphic vision systems is a good match to embedded systems because of their power and computational efficiency. However, an event-based system requires new computational approach to deal with event-data and, therefore, we developed an YOLO-based model to detect objects recorded by those cameras.


Event-based camera, You Only Look Once (YOLO) and TensorFlow
