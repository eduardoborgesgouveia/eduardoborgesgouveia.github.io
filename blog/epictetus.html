Learning to be free

Published Friday, Aug 26, 2022

Epictetus was a Greek philosopher who lived during the first and second centuries AD. He was a follower of the philosophy of Stoicism, which taught that the key to happiness and contentment lies in accepting whatever happens and not allowing oneself to be controlled by external events.

According to Epictetus, human beings are not in control of many things that happen to them in life. We do not have control over our bodies, our possessions, or the actions of others. We can only control our own thoughts and actions, and it is our responsibility to make sure that these are in line with our values and beliefs.

Free yourself from the despair of a hopeless situation and see that your fate has not been as decided as you had feared.

Mr. Nobody

One of the key insights of Stoicism is that we cannot control external events, but we can control how we respond to them. For example, if someone insults us, we have the choice of getting upset and retaliating, or of accepting the situation and not letting it affect us. By not allowing external events to control our emotions, we can maintain a sense of inner peace and tranquility.

In one of his writings, Epictetus said: "It's not what happens to you, but how you react to it that matters." This statement summarizes the Stoic philosophy of accepting and responding to life's challenges in a way that maintains our inner peace and well-being.

The thoughts of Epictetus can be applied to the daily life of a tech employee in several ways. For example, a tech employee might encounter a variety of challenges and obstacles in their work, such as technical problems, tight deadlines, or difficult coworkers.

With the Stoic philosophy of Epictetus in mind, the tech employee can focus on what they can control, such as their own thoughts and actions, and not worry about things that are outside of their control, such as the actions of others or the outcome of a particular project. This can help the tech employee to maintain a sense of inner peace and focus on the task at hand.

Additionally, the Stoic philosophy of Epictetus emphasizes the importance of living in accordance with one's values and beliefs. This can be particularly relevant for a tech employee, who may be faced with ethical dilemmas or decisions that conflict with their personal values. By staying true to their values and beliefs, the tech employee can act with integrity and maintain their sense of self-respect.

Overall, the philosophy of Epictetus can provide a useful framework for tech employees to navigate the challenges and obstacles that they may encounter in their work, and to maintain a sense of inner peace and well-being.

The thoughts of Epictetus can be applied in the process of learning something new in several ways. First, the Stoic philosophy of accepting whatever happens and not allowing oneself to be controlled by external events can be helpful in learning a new skill or subject.

For example, when faced with a difficult concept or problem, it can be tempting to get frustrated or overwhelmed. However, with the Stoic philosophy in mind, the learner can remind themselves that they cannot control the difficulty of the material, but they can control how they respond to it. This can help the learner to stay calm and focused, and to approach the learning process with a positive attitude.

Additionally, the Stoic emphasis on living in accordance with one's values and beliefs can be relevant in the learning process. For example, if the learner has a strong belief in the value of hard work and persistence, they can use this belief to motivate themselves to persevere through challenges and obstacles in their learning.

Overall, the philosophy of Epictetus can provide a useful framework for learners to approach the process of learning something new with a positive attitude and a focus on what they can control.
