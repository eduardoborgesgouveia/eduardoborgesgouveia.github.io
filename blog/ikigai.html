Ikigai and tech minds

Published Friday, Oct 7, 2022

Ikigai, a Japanese concept that roughly translates to "reason for being," is often used to describe the intersection of what one loves, what one is good at, what the world needs, and what one can be paid for. In the world of tech jobs, ikigai can be a crucial factor in determining job satisfaction and overall happiness for young minds entering the workforce.

I think thta, for many young people in tech, finding their ikigai can be a daunting task. The fast-paced and constantly evolving nature of the industry can make it difficult to pinpoint what one is truly passionate about and what the world truly needs. Additionally, the pressure to excel and advance in the field can lead to burnout and a loss of motivation.

However, when young minds are able to align their passions and skills with the needs of the world and a viable career path, the potential for success and fulfillment in a tech job is limitless. For example, a young programmer who is passionate about environmental sustainability and skilled in developing clean energy technology can find ikigai in a job at a renewable energy startup.

Furthermore, the relationship between young minds and work in tech can also be mutually beneficial. As younger generations bring fresh perspectives and innovative ideas to the table, they can help push the industry forward and address pressing issues such as diversity and inclusion. In turn, tech companies can offer opportunities for growth and development to young minds, helping them to hone their skills and find their ikigai.

Overall, while the search for ikigai in the world of tech can be challenging, it is an important and rewarding journey for young minds. By aligning their passions, skills, and career goals with the needs of the world, they can not only find fulfillment in their work but also make a positive impact on the industry and society as a whole.
